Grooming tips for men:

1. Growing, removing or keeping facial hair is a necessity. Anyone who has trouble doing any of the above can find our grooming tips helpful.

For maintaining facial hair:

-Always trim. Even if your facial hair is less than two inches thick, trimming your facial hair helps prevent irritation, itchiness and promotes evenness.
-When shaving, depending on what you want left over, always use an electric razor and/or scissors if your beard is large so your shave is more efficient.


2. For fast facial hair growers who don't want to lather on shaving cream:

-Use conditioner or oil. This helps greatly reduce razor burns/bumps (reddening of the skin) due to the fact that conditioner seeps into your skin and softens up your hair follicles before you shave.
-Otherwise, if you only shave thick beards, use shaving cream or shaving gel.


3. Relating to the last point:

-Before you apply that conditioner or shaving cream, ALWAYS apply a hot towel or have a hot shower to ensure your hair follicles are easier to shave. Otherwise, a dry shave may result in an awkward, irritable shave and therefore increase the risk of razor burns.


4. An important must for anyone looking for sooth skin:

-Exfoliate before you shave. Or, if your beard is thick, cut most of your beard and then exfoliate before shaving.


5. Shave every OTHER day. Shaving two days in a row is fine but doing so regularly could increase the risk of razor burns or scarring (although this is very unlikely it is always good to avoid).
